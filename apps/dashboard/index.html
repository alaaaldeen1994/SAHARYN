Real-Time Data Sources

We watch your assets
from space.

We don't rely on local weather reports or ground sensors alone. SAHARYN maintains three continuously active uplinks to the world's most trusted satellite agencies — giving you environmental intelligence that simply cannot be gathered any other way.

Bridge 01 — NASA
MODIS Aerosol Tracking
Two NASA satellites — Terra and Aqua — orbit Earth every 99 minutes. We tap their MODIS sensors to get Aerosol Optical Depth (AOD) readings: a precise measure of how much dust and particulate matter is in the air above your site. This data is critical for predicting filter blockages and compressor stress 48 hours ahead.
DATA PRODUCT MOD04_L2 / MOD08_D3
Bridge 02 — ESA
Sentinel-2 Plume Detection
The ESA Sentinel-2 satellite captures 13 spectral bands at 10-metre resolution. Our AI processes specific infrared channels to compute a Dust Detection Index (DDI) — identifying incoming sandstorm fronts that are completely invisible to standard cameras and local sensors.
RESOLUTION 10-metre precision
Bridge 03 — ESA / EU
Copernicus CAMS Atmosphere
The EU's Copernicus Atmosphere Monitoring Service (CAMS) provides certified, physics-calibrated data for wind speed, temperature, humidity, and atmospheric chemistry. We use this as the "ground truth" reference layer that all our AI physics models are validated against.
STANDARD ISO 14064-3 / GHG Protocol
All three data bridges are active and verified right now. Every satellite reading is automatically recorded in a tamper-proof audit log linked to your dashboard — no manual input required.

The Process

From satellite to action
in under one second.

SAHARYN does not just collect data — it makes decisions. Here is exactly what happens from the moment a satellite captures a reading to the moment your operations team receives an alert.

01
Satellite Captures
NASA and ESA satellites pass over your site and record atmospheric conditions — AOD, wind, surface temperature, dust plume signatures.
02
AI Fuses & Calibrates
SAHARYN combines all three data streams using a physics-based model (Reynolds number, Arrhenius kinetics) to create a single Dust Severity Index (DSI).
03
Causal Engine Propagates
A Bayesian network maps how that DSI score will move through your asset graph — filter → compressor → rotor → output — predicting where the failure will occur.
04
Prescriptions Delivered
The system generates a prioritised list of actions with ROI estimates, writes a carbon credit claim to the ledger, and alerts your team — all within 0.4ms.
