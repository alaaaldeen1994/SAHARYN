Foundations

To truly master the IS-CHRP environment, one must understand the underlying math of the **Langevin Dynamics Engine**. Our engine approximates the movement of cells in the 12-dimensional gene space as a series of Brownian steps constrained by a potential landscape.

01. Interaction Kinetics (Hill Functions)

The "Reprogramming factors" work by applying a biased velocity to specific genes in the matrix. When you select **OSKM**, the engine applies a strong positive scalar to the *OCT4* and *SOX2* genes while applying a repressive repressor vector to the *SOMATIC* gene cluster. These interactions follow a sigmoidal Hill-function:

Activation = (Factor^n) / (K^n + Factor^n)
Repression = 1 / (1 + (Factor/K)^n)

02. Epigenetic Barriers & Noise

In a healthy somatic cell, the "barriers" between the somatic valley and the pluripotent valley are high. The **Genetic Noise** slider effectively "heats up" the system, increasing the random displacement component (Γ). As noise increases, cells gain enough thermal energy to jump over these barriers spontaneously—recreating the phenomenon of stochastic reprogramming efficiency.

03. The Oncogenic Hazard Model

Tumorogenesis in IS-CHRP is modeled as a failure of the **Genomic Stability** threshold. When the mutation slider is active, cells have a probabilistic chance of a "Losing state" in their DNA-repair gene. If this happens, the *c-MYC* gene enters a runaway feedback loop, triggering the red pulsing Malignant visual state.
